Interactive Lesson

Learn to Play DIG UP Adventure

A treasure-hunting card game by Babel Dread & Régis Torres

2–4 Players 15–30 Min Ages 8+
Step 1

The Goal

You are an adventurer digging for gems in an ancient mine. Your mine is a 3×3 grid of nine cards. At the start, eight of these are face-down—you don't know what's buried there.

Your goal is simple: fill all nine slots of your mine with cards that score the most points. When any player reveals all nine of their cards, the game ends and scores are tallied.

You win by having the highest score when the game ends. Finishing first doesn't guarantee victory—it just triggers the end.

DIG UP Adventure — game components. Photo: Neo Ludis
1. Official Rulebook: "The goal is to fill the 9 slots of your mine with cards granting you the most points."
2. Board Game Arena: "As soon as a Mine is entirely revealed, the game comes to an end."
Game Comparison

If you've played Skyjo or the card game Golf, you'll feel right at home. Dig Up Adventure uses the same hidden-grid-of-cards concept, but adds adventure cards for more player interaction.

Common Mistake

Don't race to reveal all your cards first! Completing your mine triggers the end, but the highest score wins. A mine full of bad cards loses to one with carefully placed gems.

Step 2

How Scoring Works

Before learning what to do on your turn, you need to understand what you're optimizing for. Every card in your mine scores based on two factors: color and position.

Your Favorite Color

At the start of the game, you receive a Player Card that assigns you a favorite color. Gems matching your favorite color are worth more.

Position Numbering

Each slot in your mine is numbered 1 through 9. Each gem card is also numbered 1–9. When a gem's number matches the slot it's placed in, you score a position bonus.

The maximum score for a single card is 3 points — your favorite color in its matching numbered position. A perfect mine of all 9 favorite-color gems in the right spots would score 27 points.

1. BGA Rules: "Favorite color in correct position = 3 points, favorite color incorrect position = 2 points, non-favorite color correct position = 1 point."
2. Philibert product page: "Idol cards = 2 points. Skeleton cards = no points."
Common Mistake

Many new players focus only on color and ignore the position numbers entirely. A gem of your color in the right slot is worth 50% more than one in the wrong slot (3 vs 2). Always check the number!

Strategy Tip

A non-favorite gem in its correct position (1 pt) is better than nothing. If you can't find your color, position-matching is your fallback plan.

Step 3

What You Do on Your Turn

Your turn has three phases, always in this order:

1Play Cards
2Discard
3Draw
↻Next Player

Phase 1: Play Up to 2 Cards

You may play 0, 1, or 2 cards from your hand. You can play any combination of gem cards and adventure cards.

Phase 2: Discard (Optional)

After playing, you may discard any unwanted cards from your hand to the discard pile.

Phase 3: Draw Back to 3

Refill your hand to 3 cards from the draw pile. Your hand size is always 3 at the start of your next turn.

Your hand limit is 3 cards. You always draw back up to 3 at the end of your turn, regardless of how many you played or discarded.

1. BGA Rules: "On your turn, you can play up to 2 cards from your hand."
2. BGA Rules: "Hand limit is 3. Your hand will automatically be filled back up to 3."
Key Insight

You don't have to play any cards. Sometimes it's better to discard a bad hand and draw fresh. Playing a card just to play one can backfire.

Common Mistake

Some players try to draw cards mid-turn. Drawing only happens at the end, after you've played and discarded.

Step 4

Playing Gem Cards

Gem cards are the foundation of your mine. When you play a gem card from your hand, you place it into a slot in your mine. The result depends on whether the target slot is face-down or face-up.

Onto a Face-Down Slot

When you play a gem onto a face-down card, the two cards swap. Your gem goes into the mine (face-up), and the face-down card comes into your hand — now revealed to you.

Gem from hand → Swap → Old card to hand

Onto a Face-Up Slot

When you play a gem onto a slot that already has a face-up card, you replace it. The old card goes to the discard pile (not your hand).

Gem from hand → Replace → Old card to discard

You cannot peek at your own face-down cards. The only way to see what's hidden in your mine is to swap a gem into that slot, or use an adventure card that lets you peek or reveal.

1. BGA News: "Deploy Gem cards into their mines and utilize Adventure cards for strategic advantages."
2. Meeple QC Review: Confirmed swap mechanic and discard behavior for face-up replacements.
Common Mistake

New players often think swapping a face-down card discards it. It goes to your hand! This is a key tactical difference — you now know that card and can plan around it.

Game Comparison

This swap-and-reveal mechanic is almost identical to Skyjo and Golf. The twist is that Dig Up Adventure cares about which slot a card lands in, not just its value.

Strategy Tip

Early game, swap into face-down slots to discover what's hidden. Later, replace face-up cards to optimize your score. The swap phase is intel-gathering; the replace phase is fine-tuning.

Step 5

Adventure Cards

Adventure cards are action cards with special effects. When you play one, you apply its effect immediately, then discard it. Adventure cards never go into your mine—they're single-use tools.

What Adventure Cards Do

Adventure cards let you manipulate the game state in ways gem cards can't. Their effects fall into five categories:

ActionEffect
RevealFlip a face-down card in any player's mine face-up
PeekSecretly look at a face-down card (yours or an opponent's) without revealing it to others
SwapExchange cards between mines — yours or between opponents
StealTake a card from an opponent's mine and replace it with one from yours
DrawDraw extra cards from the draw pile

Each adventure card describes its specific effect. Read the card text carefully when you play it.

Adventure cards are discarded after use. They never occupy a mine slot. Play them for their effect, then put them in the discard pile.

1. BGA News: "Use Adventure cards to your advantage to reveal, swap or steal cards in play."
2. 1jour-1jeu: "Action cards help you progress or even trouble your adversaries, and are discarded immediately."
Game Comparison

Adventure cards serve a similar role to action cards in Uno—they let you mess with opponents or give yourself an advantage beyond just playing gems.

Strategy Tip

Peek cards are underrated. Knowing what's face-down in your mine lets you make smarter swaps later. Information is power!

Step 6

Idols & Skeletons

Two special card types can appear in your mine alongside gems.

Idol Cards ★

Idols are golden treasures worth 2 points in any position. They ignore the position-numbering system—an idol scores 2 points no matter where it sits.

You may play an idol from your hand into your mine, but only if you don't already have one in your mine. However, adventure card effects (like swaps or steals) can result in multiple idols in your mine.

Skeleton Cards ☠

Skeletons are duds worth 0 points. They occupy a mine slot without contributing to your score. If one turns up in your mine, try to replace it as soon as possible.

You can only play 1 idol from your hand into your mine. If your mine already contains an idol (even one placed by a card effect), you cannot play another from your hand. Multiple idols only happen through adventure card effects.

1. BGA Rules: "Idol cards = 2 points." and "You can't play an idol card from your hand if there is already one in your mine."
2. BGA Rules: "Skeleton cards = no points."
Common Mistake

Players sometimes think you can never have more than one idol. You can—just not from your hand. Swaps and steals can land extra idols in your mine, and that's perfectly legal.

Strategy Tip

An idol at 2 pts is better than a wrong-color gem in the wrong slot (0 pts), but worse than your favorite color in any position (2–3 pts). Use idols to plug gaps, not as a primary strategy.

Step 7

Game End & Winning

The game ends the moment any player has all 9 cards in their mine face-up. This can happen through gem swaps, adventure card reveals, or any combination.

Once triggered, all players flip their remaining face-down cards and calculate their scores.

Mine complete → All mines revealed → Tally scores → Highest wins!

Tiebreaker

If two or more players tie on points, the player with the most consecutive cards of their favorite color in their mine wins. Count adjacent slots (reading left-to-right, top-to-bottom) that contain your color.

When any mine is fully revealed, the game ends immediately. Every remaining face-down card in all mines is flipped and scores are tallied. You don't get extra turns.

DIG UP Adventure — box art by Régis Torres. Photo: 1jour-1jeu
1. BGA News: "As soon as a Mine is entirely revealed, the game comes to an end and the player with the most points wins!"
2. BGA Rules: "Tie breaker: the person with the most consecutive cards of their favorite color wins."
Common Mistake

Rushing to reveal all 9 cards is tempting, but dangerous. If your mine is full of skeletons and wrong-color gems, you're handing the win to opponents who took time to optimize.

Key Insight

The endgame trigger creates a fascinating tension: you want to optimize your mine, but if you wait too long, an opponent might end the game before you're ready.

Step 8

Setup

Now that you understand how the game works, setup is straightforward.

  1. Give each player a Player Card that shows their favorite color. Keep it visible.
  2. Shuffle all Gem Cards into one deck and all Adventure Cards into a separate deck. Place both as face-down draw piles in the center.
  3. Deal 9 cards face-down from the Gem deck to each player.
  4. Each player arranges their 9 cards into a 3×3 grid — this is their Mine. All cards stay face-down.
  5. Each player flips their center card (position 5) face-up.
  6. Each player draws 3 cards to form their starting hand.
  7. Choose a starting player. Play clockwise.
Starting Player Setup
1. BGA Rules: "Each player receives 9 unknown cards and set up their Mine by placing them face down in a 3x3 square."
2. BGA Rules: "They flip the center card face up and the game can begin."
Key Insight

Notice that setup comes after understanding the rules. By now, you know that position 5 is the center and why seeing that card matters — it's your first piece of information about your mine.

Speed Tip

Deal 9 cards to each player in a row, then let each person arrange their own grid. It's faster than dealing one card at a time into a grid formation.

Step 9

Complete Turn Summary

Here's everything in one place. You're ready to play!

1. Play up to 2 cards — Gem cards go into your mine (swap with face-down or replace face-up). Adventure cards apply their effect and are discarded. Idols fill slots for 2 pts each.
2. Discard — Optionally discard any unwanted cards from your hand.
3. Draw back to 3 — Refill your hand to 3 cards from the draw pile.
4. Next player — Play passes clockwise. When any mine is fully revealed, the game ends.

First Game Tip

For your first game, focus on learning your mine's hidden cards through swaps. Don't worry about perfect placement yet — just get a feel for the rhythm of play-discard-draw.

Variant: Shorter Game

For younger or new players, try playing with a 2×2 mine (4 slots) using only gem cards numbered 1–4. It's a great way to learn scoring before introducing the full game.
